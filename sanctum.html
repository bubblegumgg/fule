Sanctum of Truth

A level prototype built in Unreal Engine that integrates modular architecture, narrative-driven spatial planning, and optimized lighting systems to support both exploration flow and thematic storytelling.

Level Design UE Blueprint Modular Assets
Final environment panorama, demonstrating planned lighting composition, scale, and atmosphere.

01. Architectural Composition & Scale

Lighting setup overview. Whole view.

This level is designed as a two-layer exploration space that guides the player from the visible world into a hidden, introspective environment. The upper chapel serves as the entry hub, setting the tone with open sightlines and symbolic architecture. A concealed passage behind the altar leads to an underground corridor, where the layout becomes tighter, darker, and more atmospheric to signal the shift into the subconscious layer of the journey. The path ultimately opens into a central dome chamber, the final destination of the level. Its circular geometry, vertical light shaft, and symmetrical composition create a dramatic reveal moment, reinforcing the sense of arrival and narrative resolution. The progression from chapel → corridor → dome establishes a clear special rhythm and emotional arc, supporting both exploration pacing and thematic storytelling.

04. Narrative Concept

To Find the Truth

In a distant future, human society has been worn thin by countless wounds. Many hearts cry out for release. Like others who still hold a quiet resolve, a lone traveler set out a long path of searching Truth, a miracle humanity has long forgotten.

Across countless days and nights, she crossed mountains and valleys. Them one day, by chance, she caught sight of a sanctuary hidden up deep within trees. It looks like the shadow of a lost faith, or a trial disguised as a sacred site. Yet it belongs to no religion and serves no doctrine. The doors open slowly. Dust and moss cover the interior. The pews are empety. A great stained-glass window standing in silence behind the altar. Behind the altar, she discovers a hidden door. Beyond it lies a narrow spiral passage, descending like a path from the outer world to the depths of the mind. She walks carefully; the stone steps are worn and cold.

At the end, she opens another door. A vaulted chamber reveals itself. At its center, a stone pedestal holds a round silver basin. It is empty. A single beam of sunlight falls through an opening in the dome above, casting an almost otherworldy light across the metal.

She approaches. Along the rim of the basin, in an ancient elven script, a line is engraved:

"Na i thand vi gûr"

In that moment, she understands. What she had sought so desperately had never been elsewhere. It had always lived quietly in her own heart.

All forms are but illusions.

The self is already whole.

Nothing external is needed.

the Silver Basin

The mirror of the basin: it symbolizes the idea that Truth is ultimately self-knowledge. I’ve always loved The Lord of the Rings, and to me the Elves embody wisdom and the higher nature of humanity. That’s why I chose to use Sindarin for the inscription engraved on the silver basin.

Na i thand vi gûr "Truth is in the heart"

It means: “Truth is in the heart”

Na = is i = the thand = truth vi = in

gûr = heart (in the sense of emotion, the inner self)

The inscription is carved in Tengwar Annatar, giving it the same mysterious elegance as the script in the The Lord of the Rings.

the pattern

From Surface to Essence — Symbolism & Philosophical Parallels

The contrast between the sanctum above and the chamber below forms a symbolic journey from the "outer world" to the "inner self." The upper church represents reason, order, and the surface of the human world; the underground passage embodies the subconscious, the soul, and the truths hidden deep within.

This systemic contrast draws parallels from various philosophical traditions, modeling the journey from form to essence:

  • Jungian Psychology: It mirrors the path toward individuation.
  • Daoist Thought (Zhuangzi): It is the shedding of illusions to return to one's original nature.
  • Wang Yangming's Philosophy: It echoes the inward search for innate knowledge.
  • Buddhism: This is the movement from form to essence.

Ultimately, “truth” is never found at the end of the passage, but within the heart of the one who walks it.

05. Visual Reference

Architecture of the Subconscious

To create a space that feels both historical and transcendent, I studied key features of Italian medieval monastic architecture—such as semicircular arches, cloister-like corridors, and rhythmic colonnades. However, I removed all religious imagery or iconography, preserving only the raw stone textures and clean geometric proportions. This gives the sanctuary a sense of archetypal sacredness in a Jungian sense—ancient yet neutral, mysterious yet open.

In terms of atmosphere, I borrowed the contemplative spatial qualities of monastic environments, while integrating Eastern philosophical ideas about “emptiness and fullness” and “light and shadow.” As a result, the sanctuary becomes a threshold between consciousness and the subconscious, rather than a representation of any real-world religious institution.
